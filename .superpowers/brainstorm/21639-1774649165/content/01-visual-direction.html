A — Linear Style

Clean whites, subtle grays, minimal borders. Ultra-refined with lots of whitespace. Professional and focused. Like Linear, Vercel, or Notion.

Strengths

  • Maximum clarity
  • Scales to complex data
  • Feels fast & modern

Considerations

  • Can feel cold
  • Needs careful typography

B — Stripe/Dashboard Style

Dark sidebar with light content area. Accent color (indigo/purple) for highlights. Polished and data-focused. Like Stripe, GitHub, or Supabase dashboard.

Strengths

  • Strong visual hierarchy
  • Dark sidebar anchors navigation
  • Accent colors add personality

Considerations

  • Dark mode needs careful contrast
  • More opinionated

C — Warm & Approachable

Warm off-whites, earth tones, soft shadows. Rounded corners, organic feel. Welcoming for non-technical team members. Like Notion, Cron, or Linear's warmer side.

Strengths

  • Feels human & friendly
  • Great for mixed teams
  • Stands out from cold SaaS

Considerations

  • May feel less "enterprise"
  • Warm tones harder in dark mode
